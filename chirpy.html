CHIRPY's ADVENTURE

I worked on this project during the sophomore year of highschool. Starting that year I was beginning to take Java classes, and I was eager to find out whether I had the ability to make a game on my own and improve my coding skills.

The game is the simple idea of a little bird protagonist in a platform jumping game.

I started from scratch in Java, researching first how to render sprites on screen, then setting up a grids system for the tiles of the ground and obstacles, then adding in gravity and collisions, damage checking, etc.

Hand-drawn graphics, with the protag's sprite changing depending on whether it's going up or falling down

I added in things like a spring that shoots the protag up into the air, and enemies (flies) that follow the protag and bother it non-stop

The boss was a tree that shoots leaves to attack, to which the protag could fight back by shooting music notes at it instead

Below is a video showing me going through the (only) level of my game:

When I designed the protagonist, I also considered feline and amphibian options, but eventually went with the little bird. The flower monster was inspired by the slightly creepy flowers in Disney's 1951 version of Alice in Wonderland.
